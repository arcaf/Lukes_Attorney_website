Our Approach

Most debt resolution companies use a defensive strategy which is ineffective. At the LEGAL DEBT DEFENDERS we take a different approach.

We use Federal laws such as the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) and the Federal Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) to put the law on your side by making debt collectors and junk debt buyers legally validate the debt and the amount they allege you owe to prove their legal authority or “standing” to collect from you in the first place. These laws were enacted by the U.S. Congress to help protect the rights of consumers to ensure that creditors do not abuse your rights as they try to collect old, outdated and “Junk Debt” as well as from inaccurately reporting inaccurate information regarding any such accounts to the credit reporting agencies.

We have a proven system to help you fight back and turn the tables on debt collectors and junk debt buyers who take advantage of de. Our procedure disputes the very account that debt collectors are attempting to collect. We ask the debt collector for proof and documentation to validate the debt. We require them to provide validation and proof of their legal authority to collect from you.

We use the law and investigate violations of laws such as Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) and the Federal Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) amongst others to identify any potential violations. Once potential violations have been identified we then work with seasoned legal professionals to attempt to eliminate the debts that creditors and debt collectors are unable to “validate” and help resolve the accounts which have been properly validated. Meanwhile, we will also work towards stopping unwanted collection calls according to Federal Debt Collection Practices Act § 809(b) found at 15 USC 1692g which reads in pertinent parts that “…If the consumer notifies the debt collector in writing…that the debt, or any portion thereof, is disputed, or that the consumer requests the name and address of the original creditor, the debt collector shall cease collection of the debt, or any disputed portion thereof…”
